2. Boilerplate

Use verbatim in press releases, partnership decks and About sections. Approximately 70 words.

Yander is a scenic route planner for drivers, cyclists, walkers and runners — built in the UK on Ordnance Survey data. Where most maps optimise for speed, Yander plans the route that is the destination: beyond the motorways, away from the crowds, to somewhere worth heading to. Yander launched in 2026 and is operated by Propingtons Ltd.

5. Founder bio

Approximately 50 words. Use as supplied.

Sean Leishman is the founder of Yander. He started building scenic-route software after one too many motorway weekends — and discovered the UK's most interesting roads, towpaths and bridleways were the ones no maps optimised for. Yander is built on Ordnance Survey data and operated by Propingtons Ltd.

8. Usage rules

The short version of our brand guidelines — full detail in the brand guidelines PDF.

  • Write the name as Yander, with a capital Y, in body copy, headlines, taglines, meta tags, OG titles and as a verb ("to Yander") or noun ("a Yander"). The only exception is the designed lowercase wordmark in the official lockup.
  • Clear space. Give the Y mark or lockup at least the height of the Y on every side. Don't crowd it with type, badges or other marks.
  • Don't recolour the Y. Use the supplied Mint, Ink, Paper, Amber or White versions only.
  • Don't reinstate the retired teardrop/pin emblem from earlier branding.
  • Credit Ordnance Survey on any screen or asset showing OS basemap data: Contains OS data © Crown copyright and database right 2026. See our Attribution page for the full set of required strings.
  • Tone of voice — warm, invitational, lightly literary. We don't shout, we don't use exclamation marks, and we don't lean on generic travel words like "adventure", "explore" or "journey".
