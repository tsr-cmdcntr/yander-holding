1. Who this agreement is between

"We", "us", and "Yander" mean Yander Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales. "You" means the person using the Yander app or the yander.app website.

These terms apply from the moment you create an account or open the app. If you don't agree with them, the app won't be much use to you, and you should stop using it.

2. Your account

You need an account to use Yander. To create one you must:

  • Be at least 13 years old.
  • Use an email address you actually own.
  • Not share your account credentials with anyone else.

You're responsible for what happens under your account. If you think someone else has access, email hello@yander.app and we'll help you lock it down.

3. The free tier (Wanderer)

The free tier is genuinely free, with no time limit. It includes:

  • Routes up to 50 miles in length.
  • Up to 3 saved routes.
  • Voice-guided driving with the standard OpenStreetMap basemap.
  • GPX export, with a small Yander watermark in the file's metadata.

We do not show ads on the free tier. We do not sell or share your data with advertisers. We do not impose hidden caps beyond the three above.

4. Plus and the Week Pass

Plus unlocks unlimited route length, unlimited saved routes, offline Ordnance Survey 1:25k and Open Roads mapping for all of Great Britain, themed detours, and clean (unwatermarked) GPX export.

  • Plus monthly: £3.99/month. Renews automatically each month until you cancel.
  • Plus annual: £31.99/year. Renews automatically each year until you cancel. You'll receive a renewal reminder email 14 days before each annual renewal.
  • Week Pass: £4.99 one-off, seven days of Plus, does not auto-renew. Buy it again if you want it again.

All prices include VAT at the prevailing UK rate. If we change Plus pricing, the new price only applies to renewals at least 30 days after we've notified you. You can cancel before the new price takes effect.

5. The 7-day trial

Plus is offered with a 7-day free trial, available once per customer, on the monthly or annual plan. The trial:

  • Starts the moment you tap "Start trial". You get full Plus access immediately.
  • Shows a persistent "Trial — N days left" badge in your Profile, counting down daily.
  • Triggers a reminder email on Day 5 (48 hours before the trial ends).
  • Triggers a second reminder email on Day 7 at 09:00, on the day of conversion.
  • Converts to a paid Plus membership at 23:59 on Day 7 unless you've cancelled.

You can cancel at any point during the trial. If you cancel mid-trial, you keep Plus until the trial ends, then drop back to Wanderer with your saved routes intact (limited to the first three).

6. Cancelling

You can cancel Plus from inside the app in three taps:

  1. Tap Profile.
  2. Tap Membership.
  3. Tap Cancel Plus, then confirm.

You can also cancel by emailing hello@yander.app from the email on file. We will action it the same working day.

If you bought Plus through the App Store or Google Play, you also need to cancel the subscription with Apple or Google to stop the renewal. The in-app cancel button will tell you this and link you to the right place. We are required to mention this — Apple and Google charge through their stores, not us.

We do not use retention popups. When you tap cancel, you cancel — there's no "are you sure" with a discount, no pause-instead, no exit interview. We also promise not to email you about Plus for 30 days after a cancel.

7. Refunds

14-day cooling-off period (Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013). If you bought Plus directly from yander.app, you can request a full refund within 14 days of purchase. Email hello@yander.app with your receipt and we'll refund the same working day.

If you've already downloaded offline OS map tiles and used them, the digital content has been "supplied" in the legal sense and we're not statutorily obliged to refund — but we will almost always honour the request unless we see clear abuse (multiple refund cycles, etc.).

App Store and Play Store purchases. Refunds for these are handled by Apple at reportaproblem.apple.com and Google at play.google.com. We can't refund those purchases directly, but we'll write to Apple or Google to support your claim if asked.

Nothing in this section removes your statutory rights under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 — in particular your rights when digital content is faulty, not as described, or unfit for purpose.
