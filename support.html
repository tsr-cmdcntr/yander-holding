Support

Common questions, plain answers.

Ten of the questions we hear most often. If yours isn't here, email hello@yander.app and a human will reply within two working days.

  • What is Yander, exactly?

    Yander is a route planner and turn-by-turn navigator for the UK. It works in four modes — drive, cycle, walk, run — and it's built around the idea of the scenic route, not the fastest one.

    It will plan a route between two points (or a round trip from one point), read directions out loud as you go (including in vehicle mode), show you Ordnance Survey 1:25k mapping offline if you're on Plus, and export your route as a GPX file.

    It will not show ads. It will not sell your location. It will not nag you to upgrade.

  • What's free, and what's behind Plus?

    Wanderer (free, forever): routes up to 50 miles, up to 3 saved routes, voice-guided driving with the OpenStreetMap basemap, GPX export with a small Yander watermark.

    Plus (£3.99/month or £31.99/year, 7-day free trial): unlimited route length, unlimited saved routes, offline Ordnance Survey 1:25k + Open Roads mapping, themed detours, clean GPX export.

    Week Pass (£4.99, one-off): all Plus features for seven days. Does not auto-renew. Buy it again if you want it again.

  • How does the free trial work — and how do I cancel?

    When you start the seven-day Plus trial, three things happen. A small "Trial — N days left" badge appears on your Profile screen and stays there for the entire trial. We send you an email on Day 5 reminding you the trial ends in 48 hours. We send a second email on Day 7 at 09:00 telling you the trial ends today.

    To cancel: open Yander → tap Profile → tap Membership → tap Cancel. That's three taps. You can do it at any point during the trial and you won't be charged.

    We do not show a "wait, are you sure?" screen with discounts. We do not delay the cancel. If you cancel mid-trial, you keep Plus until the trial ends, then drop back to the free tier with all your saved routes intact (limited to the first three).

  • Why does Yander need background location?

    In drive mode only, Yander asks for background location so it can keep navigating when your screen turns off, when you switch to Spotify, or when a call comes in. Without it, turn-by-turn voice guidance would stop the moment you took your eyes off the screen — which is exactly when you need it most.

    On Android, you'll see a persistent "Yander is navigating" notification while drive mode is active. Tap it to return to the map, or swipe it once you've parked.

    In cycle, walk, or run mode, Yander uses foreground-only location. You can deny background location and the app will still work — you just won't get voice guidance with the screen off in drive mode.

    We never track you when you aren't navigating. There is no analytics call, no silent ping, no "where have you been today" log. Location data stays on your device unless you choose to export a GPX file.

  • How accurate is the Ordnance Survey mapping?

    On Plus, Yander ships Ordnance Survey 1:25k Explorer mapping and Open Roads — the same data the OS Maps app uses, licensed directly from Ordnance Survey. It's the gold standard for UK terrain mapping: every footpath, bridleway, contour line, and right-of-way is on there.

    Offline tiles cover the whole of Great Britain. Northern Ireland uses OSM data (we're working on OSNI licensing for a future release).

    If you spot a mapping error, it's almost certainly in the underlying OS or OSM data, not in Yander. Report it to Ordnance Survey at osmaps.com/help or to OpenStreetMap at openstreetmap.org.

  • I want a refund. What's the process?

    If you're within 14 days of your first Plus purchase and haven't used the offline maps offline, email hello@yander.app with your receipt and we'll refund the same working day. This is your statutory right under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 and the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.

    If you've downloaded offline OS tiles and used them on a route, the digital content has been "supplied" in legal terms and we're not obliged to refund. We will still consider it case-by-case — email and explain.

    If you bought Plus through the Apple App Store, refunds are handled by Apple at reportaproblem.apple.com. If you bought through Google Play, refunds within 48 hours are handled by Google at play.google.com. After 48 hours, email us.

  • Does Yander work without signal?

    Yes, if you're on Plus and you've downloaded the area beforehand.

    Plus: pre-download any region of Great Britain. Routing, voice guidance, and OS mapping all work fully offline.

    Free: routing requires signal. The OSM basemap caches recently-viewed tiles but the 50-mile cap and three-save limit still apply.

    To download offline maps on Plus: tap Maps → Offline regions → choose a region → Download. A 50 × 50 km tile is roughly 180 MB.

  • Why don't you offer student or family pricing?

    Because we don't want to bait people with a discount and then quietly hope they forget to cancel.

    The price is the same for everyone. £3.99 a month or £31.99 a year. If that's too much, the free tier is genuinely usable — 50 miles covers most day trips and 95% of dog walks. If you want Plus for a one-off weekend, buy the £4.99 Week Pass.

  • Does Yander work with CarPlay or Android Auto?

    Not in version 1.0. We're working on it for v1.1. If you depend on car-screen mirroring, please wait for that release before paying for Plus.

    In the meantime, drive mode works on your phone screen with voice guidance reading turns out loud. We recommend a dashboard mount.

  • How do I delete my account and all my data?

    Open Yander → Profile → Settings → Delete account. Confirm with your email and we'll wipe your account, saved routes, and any cached location data within 24 hours. We do not keep "soft-deleted" copies. There is no recovery window.

    If you'd rather email us, send a deletion request to hello@yander.app from the email on file and we'll do it within two working days. You have this right under UK GDPR Article 17.

    If you only want to wipe the app from your phone but keep your account (e.g. you're reinstalling), just uninstall — your routes are synced and will be there when you sign back in.
