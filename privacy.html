Privacy Policy

What we keep, and what we don't.

Yander helps you find scenic routes. We need a little data to make that work and let you sign in. We don't take anything we don't need, and we never sell what we do.

Last updated 15 June 2026 Effective 15 June 2026 UK GDPR & EU GDPR

Yander ("Yander", "we", "us", "our") is a web application that helps you discover scenic driving, cycling and walking routes across the United Kingdom and beyond. This Privacy Policy explains what personal data we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and the rights you have under the UK GDPR and EU GDPR.

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or wish to exercise any of your rights, contact us at privacy@yander.app.

2. What data we collect

We collect the minimum data needed to operate Yander. The categories below are exhaustive — if a category is not listed here, we do not collect it.

2.1 Account information (when you sign up)

  • Email address (required, used for login and password reset).
  • Display name (optional).
  • Profile photo URL (optional; if you sign in with Google, this is the URL of your Google profile picture — we do not copy the image to our servers).
  • Account creation timestamp.

2.2 Authentication data

  • A bcrypt-hashed copy of your password (we never see or store your password in plain text).
  • For Google sign-in: a Google-issued account identifier and the email address associated with the Google account. We do not receive your Google password.

2.3 Saved content

  • Routes, plans and journeys you choose to save.
  • Preferences you set within the app (units, default mode, etc.).
  • Any waitlist sign-up email address you provide.

2.4 Device location (only while in use)

  • Your device's GPS coordinates, only while you are actively using a feature that needs them (live navigation, "use my location" search).
  • We do not store your live location on our servers. It is processed in your browser and sent to the routing service only to compute the route you requested.
  • You can refuse the browser location prompt at any time; Yander remains usable without it.

2.5 Usage and diagnostic data

  • Anonymous aggregate analytics (page views, feature usage) when you have consented via the cookie banner.
  • Server-side logs containing your IP address, request path, response status and timestamp — kept for up to 30 days for security and abuse-prevention purposes, then automatically deleted or anonymised.

2.6 Cookies and similar technologies

See Section 7 for the full cookie inventory.

5. International transfers

Our primary data store (Supabase) is hosted in the United Kingdom or European Economic Area. Some processors listed in Section 4 are based in the United States. Where personal data leaves the UK / EEA, we rely on:

  • the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, and / or
  • the EU Standard Contractual Clauses,

as approved by the UK Information Commissioner's Office and the European Commission. You may request a copy of the safeguards in place by emailing privacy@yander.app.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR and EU GDPR

If you are located in the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area you have the following rights:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your account and associated data.
  • Restriction — ask us to limit how we use your data while a query is resolved.
  • Portability — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.
  • Object — object to processing based on our legitimate interests, including any direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw any consent you previously gave (for example for marketing emails), with no effect on processing done before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@yander.app. We will respond within one month, free of charge. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK Information Commissioner's Office or your local EEA supervisory authority.

9. Data security

  • All traffic to Yander is encrypted in transit using HTTPS (TLS 1.2+).
  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt at cost factor 10 or higher.
  • Session cookies are HttpOnly, Secure and SameSite=Lax.
  • Access to the application database and server logs is restricted to the application backend and the operator; access is logged.
  • We routinely apply security updates to our dependencies and infrastructure.

No internet service can be guaranteed 100% secure. If we ever become aware of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours and notify affected users where required by law.

10. Children

Yander is not intended for children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 13. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, please contact privacy@yander.app and we will delete the account.
