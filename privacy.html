Privacy

What we keep, and what we don't.

Yander is a route planner. We need some data to plan routes and let you sign in. We don't need anything else, so we don't take it.

1. Who we are

Yander is operated by Yander Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales. We're the data controller for the information described on this page. Sean Leishman is the named contact for any privacy question.

Email privacy@yander.app for any data request. Replies usually arrive within two working days.

2. What we collect

We collect the smallest amount of data we can get away with and still make the app work. Specifically:

  • Your email address, when you create an account. We use it to sign you in, send you trial-end reminders, and reply if you contact support.
  • Your saved routes. These are stored against your account so they sync between your devices.
  • Your membership status — whether you're on the free tier, on a trial, on Plus, or on a Week Pass — and the date your trial or renewal ends.
  • Crash and error logs, anonymised, so we can fix the app when something breaks.

That's it. We don't collect your name, phone number, address, payment details (Apple, Google, or Stripe handle those), or any kind of advertising identifier.

3. How we use location

This is the part most people care about, so we'll be specific.

Location data is used on your device, in real time, to plan and navigate routes. We do not send your location to our servers. We do not log where you've been. We do not build a profile of your movements.

In drive mode, Yander asks for background location so it can keep navigating when your screen turns off or you switch to another app. On Android, you'll see a persistent "Yander is navigating" notification while drive mode is active.

In cycle, walk, and run mode, Yander uses foreground-only location. You can deny background location and the app will still work — you just won't get voice guidance with the screen off in drive mode.

If you choose to export a route as a GPX file, that file contains location data and goes wherever you send it. That's the only time your location data leaves your device.

Yander does not have any access to your location when you aren't actively navigating. There is no silent ping, no analytics call, no "where have you been today" log.

4. Why we're allowed to (lawful basis)

Under UK GDPR we have to tell you why we're allowed to process each kind of data:

  • Your email and saved routes — performance of a contract (the one you entered into by signing up to use Yander).
  • Membership status and billing dates — performance of a contract and our legal obligations under UK tax and consumer-rights law.
  • Trial reminder emails (day 5 and day 7) — performance of a contract and compliance with the Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024. These are transactional emails and you can't unsubscribe while a trial is active.
  • Crash logs — our legitimate interest in keeping the app working. You can opt out in Settings.
  • Optional marketing emails — your consent. You can withdraw it in one tap.

5. Who we share it with

We share your data with a small number of service providers we need to run the app. We don't share it with anyone else — no advertisers, no data brokers, no analytics resellers.

  • Railway (US/EU) — hosts our database and backend. UK-EU adequacy applies; EU data centres used where possible.
  • Resend (EU) — sends our transactional emails (trial reminders, receipts, support replies).
  • Cloudflare (US/EU) — serves this website, handles email routing for our inbound addresses, and provides DDoS protection.
  • Apple and Google — handle App Store and Play Store payments. We never see your card details.
  • Stripe (UK/EU) — only used if you buy Plus directly from yander.app. We never see your card details there either.
  • Ordnance Survey (UK) — we send anonymous tile-request counts as required by our OS data licence. No user identifier is attached.

We will share data with law enforcement if we're legally required to (e.g. a court order under the Investigatory Powers Act). We'd push back on anything that didn't look properly authorised, and we'd tell you afterwards unless prohibited.

6. How long we keep it

  • Your account and saved routes: as long as your account is active. If you don't sign in for 18 months, we'll email you and then delete the account if you don't reply within 30 days.
  • Membership and billing records: 7 years, because HMRC requires it.
  • Crash logs: 90 days, then automatically purged.
  • Support emails: 2 years, then automatically purged.

If you delete your account, we wipe your account, saved routes, and any cached data within 24 hours. We do not keep "soft-deleted" copies. Membership records (item 2 above) are kept the 7 years because we legally have to.

7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you can:

  • Ask for a copy of everything we hold about you. We'll send a JSON export within one month, free of charge.
  • Correct anything that's wrong. You can fix most things in Settings; email us for anything you can't.
  • Delete your account (Settings → Delete account, or email us). See above for what gets wiped and what stays.
  • Restrict or object to specific kinds of processing (e.g. tell us not to send crash logs).
  • Withdraw consent for anything we're doing on a consent basis (marketing emails).
  • Take your data with you. The JSON export above is machine-readable.

Email privacy@yander.app for any of these. We don't charge, and we don't ask you to justify the request.

8. Cookies and tracking

The Yander app does not use cookies. It contains no third-party SDKs that track you — no Google Analytics, no Facebook SDK, no Branch, no AppsFlyer, nothing.

The yander.app website uses Cloudflare Web Analytics, which is cookieless and does not fingerprint your browser. We do not use Google Analytics, advertising pixels, or any other marketing cookies. The mailing-list signup form on the homepage stores your email in our database and sends it to Resend so we can email you when we launch — see section 2.

9. Children

Yander is intended for users aged 13 and over. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone younger. If you believe a child under 13 has signed up, email privacy@yander.app and we'll delete the account.

Under-18s should use Yander with the awareness of a parent or guardian, particularly in drive mode (which assumes a licensed driver).

10. Contact and complaints

For any data question or request:

Email: privacy@yander.app
Post: Yander Ltd, London (full address provided on request)
Named controller: Sean Leishman

If you think we've handled your data badly and you can't resolve it with us, you have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint. We'd rather you came to us first, but it's your call.
